Sick • injured • orphaned wildlife

Help safely. Act promptly.

This guide is for non-dangerous wildlife. Do not approach or handle an animal that could injure you.

Text 480-220-6930

Send a clear picture and your nearest major crossroads.

First, observe

Does the animal actually need help?

Not every young animal found alone is orphaned. A parent may be nearby gathering food or waiting for people to leave.

  • Bleeding or visibly injured
  • Cold, weak, lethargic, or unresponsive
  • Unable to stand, walk, fly, or move normally
  • Covered with flies, fly eggs, or maggots
  • Caught, bitten, or carried by a cat or dog
  • In immediate danger and unable to move to safety
  • A parent is known to be dead

If a young animal appears healthy and is in a safe location, observe from a distance. Keep children and pets away and give the parent an opportunity to return.

1

Keep the animal warm

Place a heating pad set on LOW under half of the box or carrier. If needed, use a warm-water bottle wrapped in a towel. Leave part of the container unheated so the animal can move away.

Keep the animal out of direct sunlight. Keep ducklings and other young waterfowl warm and dry; do not place them in water.

The goal is gentle warmth, not heat.
2

Keep it safe and secure

Use a ventilated cardboard box or secure pet carrier lined with paper towels or a smooth, non-slip towel.

Keep the container closed in a dark, quiet area away from children and pets. Handle the animal as little as possible, and keep it secured during transport.

3

Do NOT give food or water

Do not feed the animal or give it water unless a wildlife rehabilitator specifically instructs you to do so. The wrong food can cause serious harm.

Never pour, drip, or force water into an animal's mouth or beak. Liquid can enter the lungs and cause aspiration.

Warmth, quiet, and prompt care matter more than feeding.
4

Contact Crystal promptly

Text 480-220-6930 with a clear picture and your nearest major crossroads.

If possible, include what you observed, where the animal was found, whether it can move normally, and whether it had contact with a cat or dog.

Be prepared to transport the animal unless other arrangements are made.
